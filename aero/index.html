Project Overview

I was selected for the QiDi program at Tongji University and served as a key member on the project under the guidance of Prof. Peng Qi. Our main goal is to design and build a robot system that can move faster and more flexibly and enter smaller spaces to detect damage. Our project was under the China National University Student Innovation & Entrepreneurship Development Program. I was primarily responsible for robot perception and partial system development of the robot.

As the first author, I have published two invention patents, which are in the substantive review stage:

  • Patent 1: introduces a method for detecting aircraft engine cracks based on YOLOv5.
  • Patent 2: introduces an obstacle avoidance method, system, device, and storage medium for aircraft engine defect detection robot.
